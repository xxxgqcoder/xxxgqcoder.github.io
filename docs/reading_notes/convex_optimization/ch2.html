2.5 Separating and supporting hyperplanes

2.5.1 Separating hyperplane theorem

suppose C and D is non-empty and disjoint convex set, then there mus exists \( a \ne 0 \) and \( b \) such that \( a^T x + b \le 0 \) for all \( x \in C \)

assume the distance between C and D is defined as \[ \operatorname{dist}(C, D) = \operatorname{inf}\{ ||u-v||_2 \ \big| u \in C, \ v \in D \} \] is positive. (inf means Infimum). and there exists points \( c \in C \) and \( d \in D \) achieve the minimum distance, e.g. \( ||c-d||_2 = \operatorname{dist}(C, D) \). it is satisfied if C and D is closed and one set is bounded.

define \[ a = d-c, \ b = { ||d||_2^2 - ||c||^2_2 \over 2} \] we will find that affine function \[ \begin{aligned} f(x) & = a^Tx -b \\ & = (d-c)^T x - { ||d||_2^2 - ||c||^2_2 \over 2} \\ & = (d-c)^T x - { (d-c)^T(d+c) \over 2} \\ & = (d-c)^T( x - {(d+c) \over 2} ) \end{aligned} \] separate \( C \) and \( D \).

to prove \( f \) is nonnegative on \( D \). suppose there is a point \( u \) in \( D \) such that \[ f(u) = (d-c)^T( u - {(d+c) \over 2} ) \lt 0 \] rewrite \( f(u) \) as \[ \begin{aligned} f(u) & = (d-c)^T( u - d + (d-c)/2) \\ & = (d-c)^T(u-d) + ||d-c||^2_2/2 \\ \end{aligned} \] if \( f(u) \lt 0 \) holds, then we will have \( (d-c)^T(u-d) \lt 0 \), now observe that \[ \begin{aligned} & \frac{d}{dt} || d + t(u-d) - c||^2_2 \\ = & \frac{d}{dt} \ \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left[ t^2(u_i - d_i)^2 + 2t(u_i-d_i)(d_i-c_i) + (d_i-c_i)^2 \right] \\ = & \frac{d}{dt} \left[ t^2 ||u-d||^2_2 + 2t (d-c)^T(u-d) + ||d-c||^2_2 \right] \\ = & 2t ||u-d||^2_2 + 2(d-c)^T(u-d) \\ & \ \\ & \frac{d}{dt} || d + t(u-d) - c||^2_2 \Big|_{t=0} = 2(d-c)^T(u-d) \lt 0 \end{aligned} \] so for small \( t \in (0, 1] \) we will have \[ ||d + t(u-d) - c||_2 \lt ||d-c||_2 \] which means point \( d + t(u-d) \) is closer to c that d is. and since \( D \) is convex and \( d + t(u-d) = (1-t)d + tu \in D \). this leads to conflict since \( d \) is assumed to be the point in \( D \) that is closet to \( c \) NOTE: \( t \in (0, 1] \) is just to prove convex combination of \( u \) and \( d \) is in \( D \), actually \( t \) can take a narrow range such as \( (0, \epsilon) \) where \( \epsilon \) is a small value but less than 1
